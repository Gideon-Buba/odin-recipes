Moi-moi

Description

Moi moi is a popular Nigerian dish made from steamed bean pudding. It is made from ground peeled beans, blended with a mixture of peppers, onions, and spices, then steamed in leaves or foil until firm.

The resulting dish is a soft, spongy, savory pudding with a slightly nutty flavor. It is often served as a side dish or snack, and is a staple food in many Nigerian households. It can be eaten on its own or paired with other dishes, such as rice, stew, or bread.

Moi moi is a protein-rich food and is a good source of essential vitamins and minerals. It is also gluten-free and can be easily adapted to suit different dietary needs or preferences. In Nigerian cuisine, it is often paired with other dishes such as Jollof rice, fried rice, or bean porridge.

Ingredients

  • Black-eyed beans or brown beans
  • Onion
  • Red bell pepper
  • Scotch bonnet pepper or other hot pepper (optional)
  • Salt
  • Bouillon cube or seasoning powder
  • Palm oil or vegetable oil
  • Water
  • Foil or moi moi leaves (dried or fresh)

Steps

  1. Soak the beans: Soak the beans in water overnight or for at least 4 hours to soften them.
  2. Prepare the ingredients: Finely chop the onion and peppers, and set aside. Also, measure out the salt, bouillon cube, and oil.
  3. Peel the beans: Drain the beans and remove the skins by rubbing them gently between your palms. Rinse the beans in water to remove any remaining skins.
  4. Blend the beans: In a blender or food processor, blend the peeled beans to a smooth paste, adding a small amount of water as needed to facilitate blending.
  5. Add the seasoning: Add the chopped onion, peppers, salt, bouillon cube or seasoning powder, and oil to the blended beans. Mix everything together thoroughly.
  6. Prepare the containers: If using foil, cut the foil into squares and lightly oil them. If using moi moi leaves, soak them in water until pliable, then fold them into small cups or pouches.
  7. Fill the containers: Fill the prepared foil or leaves with the bean mixture, filling them about two-thirds full.
  8. Steam the moi moi: Place the filled containers in a large pot or steamer basket, and add enough water to the pot to create steam. Steam the moi moi over medium heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until firm.
  9. Serve the moi moi: Remove the steamed moi moi from the containers and serve warm. It can be eaten on its own or paired with other dishes. Enjoy!
